A study on Ember’s logomark.

As well as being my favourite frontend framework, I feel Ember’s branding is one of the strongest compared to it’s peers, but that doesn’t mean it can’t be improved upon.

In the spirit of open source I present my study of the Ember logo and my suggestions for improvements.

My background is in design, and I may have spent part of my youth writing letters on walls, so I feel I have the experience necessary to breakdown the current logo and find improvements in the letterforms and how they flow and fit together.

So, to begin, lets analyse the current logo.

Original iCiel Pacifico typeface

The logo is based on the iCiel Pacifico typeface. The typeface emulates handwriting, but one of the drawbacks of handwriting based fonts is, without adding a large amount of ligatures, the connections between letters can only ever be approximate. Most of the time this works out, but as I will go on to show, this causes flow problems, especially around the be in ember.

Original Ember logo

This didn’t go unnoticed by the designer of the current logo, they did a stellar job crafting a bespoke logo for the project, many modifications were made on top of the typeface.

Typeface overlaid on logo to highlight differences.

When we overlay the typeface on the logo we can see these modifications.

Broadly speaking these modifications are

  • e

    has a tail added to balance out the tail on the r

  • m

    has had it’s form tweaked, the first stem has been put on a diet to match the general weight of the stroke

  • b

    has the most tweaks, the cut out was removed, the ascender was tightened up and the connector redrawn

  • e

    some tightening up of the form

  • r

    redraw the tail to round it off and tighten it up

The flow problem.

If you were to imagine the way your hand would write out the cursive here, we run into problems around the lowercase B character joining the lowercase E. Whilst the tweaks above made improvements on the original letterforms, it is actually impossible to write out with simple, uninterrupted line work, as highlighted below.

The connector from the m does not flow into the ascender of the b.

The connector from the b does not flow into the crossbar of the e.

More tweaks!

As I feel the branding is already strong, we only need to make small tweaks to the logo to improve the flow of the letterforms.

I have redrawn the b from scratch to better fit the logo, match the angle of the other letters, and to flow correctly, as it would do if writing by hand. I have tweaked every letter in some way to keep everything consistent.
